Challenger

Setup

Use a Lego piece for each player. Choose a player to go first. Place pieces at start. Use a deck of standard playing cards and your own Challenge cards made from 3x5 cards. The Challenges are to be numbered A1-A10, B1-B10, C1-C10, and D1-D10. For a shorter game, use only the A Challenges. The face card values are on the board. They’re the same as War. The Challenges should include knocking over towers made of Lego blocks, getting the piece to a certain square, and making the piece go down stairs.

Play

The first player draws a card and moves that many spaces. Jokers are not used in this game. If you land on a challenge space (the number on the space ends in 5 or 0), draw a challenge card. Place a Lego piece on the space where the lane starts. If you land on a multiple of 5, draw a challenge card and follow the instructions on the challenge card. The numbers on the cards should be 1-10 (or whatever you choose) #A3 is the challenge where you flick the piece from lane 1 to lane 2. If it doesn’t go all the way to lane 2, you still get points (the numbers in lane 1). For knocking over a tower, the number of points you earn is the value of the space your piece lands on plus a number of points (see the “Tower points” table)

Winning

When a player (P) gets back to the start space, the game is over. The player who has the most points from completing the challenges wins. If nobody has any challenge points, P wins.

Challenger Lite

Setup

Place all the challenge cards face-up in a pile.

Play

This variation is like Challenger, except you don’t use the playing cards. Don’t take turns, just draw challenge cards. This results in a fast-paced game where everyone is trying to get the highest number of points. You can even have challenge battles, where one player (P1) is trying to get his piece into the other person (P2)’s lane and knock over a tower. But here’s the catch: If the tower is all the way in P2’s lane, P2 gets the points; halfway, divide the points up between P1 and P2; all the way in P1’s lane, P1 gets the points.

Winning

When you run out of challenge cards, the game is over. The player who has the most points from completing the challenges wins.
